The cruise industry in Saudi is still a relatively new concept and Cruise Saudi is responsible for both the development and operational elements of the ports and terminals: which is not usual practice within the wider sector. AROYA Cruises will be run as a separate business unit to the rest of the work that Cruise Saudi carries out. For example, there will be different operational and management teams and it will be separate to Destination Experiences. We are very proud of the relationships we have fostered with global cruise lines and these partnerships are of top priority to us as well as the overall vision of Cruise Saudi. With this in mind, AROYA Cruises will work cohesively with partners, rather than competitively. It is the goal of Cruise Saudi to welcome 1.3 million passengers by 2035 and this can only be achieved by all working together.

By creating a dedicated cruise experience in Saudi, for the people of Saudi, we are driving local tourism revenue back into the economy and encouraging nationals, expatriates and regional guests to explore what’s on their doorstep. AROYA Cruises will showcase the very best Arabian experiences that Saudi has to offer whilst contributing growth to the national goal of welcoming over 100 million visitors annually by 2030.

Our aim is to ensure the majority of the crew speak Arabic to reflect the nature and ethos of AROYA Cruises. Cruise Saudi is committed to our goal of providing 50,000 direct and indirect jobs in Saudi by 2035.

The launch of AROYA Cruises’ will be led by Ghassan Khan. The brand will be managed as a separate business unit supporting the fulfilment of Cruise Saudi’s strategic goals of welcoming 1.3 million cruise visitors by 2035 and providing 50,000 direct and indirect jobs.

As the Chief Strategy Officer for Cruise Saudi, Ghassan Khan brings more than 24 years of professional experience in banking, consulting, and entertainment. After starting his career in management consulting in the United States, Ghassan has overseen transformational programs for several financial institutions, such as, the National Commercial Bank, Bank Al-Jazira, and NCB Capital. Ghassan also served at the Economic Cities and Special Zones Authority as the City Development Director for King Abdullah Economic City where he supervised the development of several key socio-economic initiatives, such as, King Abdullah Port amongst others. Most recently, he was the Chief Strategy and VRO Officer at the General Entertainment Authority, where he played a key role in establishing programmes designed to develop the local entertainment sector.
